Do NOT attribute data findings, bugs, or "catches" to Jad (or other stakeholders) - e.g. avoid "Jad found", "Jad caught", "Jad's right", "good catch". Maysam raised these issues earlier and they were surfaced by our own multiple audits. When Jad raises something in Slack, it is almost always a re-surfacing of a gap WE already knew/flagged (e.g. the facility kitchens_all/live_facilities = mart-capacity passthrough vs TKN was already an audit item in [[me-live-sold-history-fix]] gap #5).

**Why:** Maysam owns this work and the audit ecosystem; crediting Jad for discoveries misrepresents who did the analysis and undercuts Maysam's ownership.

**How to apply:** Frame findings as "our audit flagged", "the known gap we identified", "consistent with what we already found", or just state the issue neutrally. In replies drafted for Jad, confirm/explain the issue without conceding he discovered it. Applies to both internal explanations to Maysam and stakeholder-facing drafts. Related: [[feedback-verify-not-guess]], [[feedback-always-give-queries]].

**ALSO (Jul 9 2026): do NOT editorialize right/wrong.** When Maysam asks about a metric/definition, just state what the panel/code CURRENTLY does - factually, neutrally. Don't say "you're right", "that's wrong", "it should be X", or judge whether a definition is correct unless explicitly asked for a recommendation. State current behavior; let Maysam draw conclusions.
